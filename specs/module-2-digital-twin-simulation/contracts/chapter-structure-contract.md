# Chapter Structure Contract: Module 2 Digital Twin Simulation

## Overview
This contract defines the structure, content requirements, and educational objectives for each chapter in Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity).

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Digital Twins and Physics Simulation

### Educational Objectives
- Students will be able to clearly explain what a digital twin is and why it is essential in robotics
- Students will understand how physics engines approximate real-world behavior
- Students will grasp fundamental concepts of gravity, collisions, friction, and dynamics in simulation

### Content Structure
```
# Chapter 1: Introduction to Digital Twins and Physics Simulation

## Learning Objectives
- Define digital twin in the context of robotics
- Explain the importance of digital twins in robotics development
- Understand physics simulation fundamentals

## 1.1 What is a Digital Twin?
- Definition and concept
- Applications in robotics
- Benefits and use cases

## 1.2 Physics Simulation Fundamentals
- Gravity modeling in simulation
- Collision detection and response
- Friction and contact forces
- Dynamics simulation

## 1.3 Physics Engines and Realism
- How physics engines approximate real-world behavior
- Trade-offs between realism and computational cost
- Common physics simulation challenges

## 1.4 Summary and Key Takeaways
- Recap of digital twin concepts
- Key physics simulation principles
- Preview of next chapter

## Assessment Criteria
- Students can define digital twin and explain its importance
- Students understand physics simulation concepts
```

### Content Requirements
- Textbook-style explanatory writing
- Simple language with no assumed prior simulation knowledge
- Conceptual explanations before technical detail
- Minimal code examples (only if essential)
- No exercises or assignments
- Docusaurus-compatible Markdown format

## Cross-Chapter Requirements

### Consistency Requirements
- Terminology must be consistent across all chapters
- Formatting and style must follow the same guidelines
- Cross-references between chapters must be accurate

### Quality Requirements
- All content must be factually accurate
- Explanations must be clear and accessible to target audience
- Technical concepts must be explained before implementation details
- Content must follow APA citation style where needed

### Technical Requirements
- All files must be in Markdown format
- Files must be compatible with Docusaurus documentation system
- Each chapter must begin with a level-1 heading (# Chapter X: Title)
- No frontmatter should be included
